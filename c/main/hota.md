# A-share Hot Sector Identification Handbook

> Companion file: [framed.md](framed.md) (A-share short-term swing discipline: gate / select / entry / exit / size).
> This handbook answers the question that precedes framed.md §2 (main-line stock selection): "How do I identify
> the current main-line sector first?"

> **[For AI assistant — offensive / profit-seeking stance]** When operating in offensive mode: prefer §7 leaders over
> followers (followers break boards more often and lag the leader's gains), and always identify which §8
> driver template is active today (policy / earnings / overseas-mapping / cycle / sanctions / meeting) — the
> template sets the realistic cadence, duration, and take-profit horizon. Do not hand me a follower when the
> leader is still enterable, and do not propose an entry without naming the template and the sector stage (§1).

---

## 0. Why A-shares require sector-first, stock-second

US-stock selection can be "bottom-up" (just clean fundamentals + perfect chart). A-shares cannot. Three
structural reasons:

1. **Sector effect is far stronger than in US markets**: 60-70% of an A-share's move is driven by its
   sector (industry beta dominates). No matter how good the single chart looks, if the sector is in
   ebb or has been abandoned by capital, it will bleed lower anyway.
2. **Policy & theme driven**: Almost every A-share leader launches alongside a policy catalyst or theme
   eruption; a "counter-main-line" stock almost never sustains an independent run.
3. **Capital is concentrated and emotional**: Northbound + mutual funds + hot money simultaneously focus
   on a handful of main lines; everything else suffers liquidity drought.

**Conclusion**: Identify the current main-line sectors first (<= 3), then pick leader / second-tier within
those, and finally apply framed.md's filter (§2 selection + §3 entry trigger). If the sector pick is wrong, no amount of stock-level
refinement saves it.

## 1. The 5-stage life cycle of a hot sector

Borrowed from Weinstein's 4 stages, but a sector has an extra "incubation" stage on top (because it
takes time for capital consensus to form):

| Stage | Characteristics | Capital movement | Action |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|--------|
| **Incubation** | 1-2 names limit up unexpectedly; sector index hasn't launched; no media coverage | Hot money probing; no institutions | Observe; do not participate |
| **Launch** | Leader on 2-3 consecutive boards; sector index breaks above 60-day MA on volume; second tier follows | Northbound / mutual funds start flowing in | **Best entry window** |
| **Main-up** | Sector index slope is steep; broad rally; new leaders relay daily | Sustained inflows; margin balance rising | Realize gains on existing; new positions need tighter stops |
| **Climax** | Media headlines / forum heat off the charts / retail search spike / sell-side analysts collectively raise targets | Retail net inflow; institutions start trimming | Scale out in tranches; no relays |
| **Ebb** | Leader fails the board / one-line limit-down / max board count collapses / northbound outflow | Capital outflow; margin unwinds | Clear the rest |

Remember: **enter ONLY in the "Launch" stage; ride through "Main-up" but open no new positions; in
"Climax / Ebb", exit strictly**.

## 2. Five-dimensional identification (capital / price / theme / sentiment / supply chain)

A real hot sector confirms across at least **3 dimensions** simultaneously. Single-dimension signal =
fake heat / one-day wonder.

### Dimension A: Capital flow (the hardest signal)

By credibility, high to low:

1. **Northbound top inflows by industry**: 3-5 consecutive days of net inflow >= 500M CNY/day into one
   industry = strong signal. Northbound is the smartest money in A-shares — institutional + long-bias.
   Following it rarely goes badly wrong.
2. **Mutual fund position change** (after quarterly / semi-annual disclosure): 2 consecutive quarters
   of >= 2pct weight increase in a sector = trend signal. Lagging but authoritative.
3. **LHB (Dragon-Tiger List) institutional seats**: A name with >= 2 institutional seats net-buying for
   2-3 consecutive sessions (>= 30M each) = institutional entry signal. Paired with broad sector rally
   = sector-level institutional consensus.
4. **Main-force capital net inflow** (THS / EastMoney definition): sector net inflow >= 2B CNY for 3
   consecutive days = capital consensus. Note: this metric includes hot money — less credible than
   northbound — pair with other signals.
5. **Margin balance change**: sector margin balance up >= 5% over 5 consecutive sessions = leveraged
   capital rushing in. Double-edged: early-stage helpful; at highs it's a precursor to ebb.
6. **ETF subscription/redemption**: sector ETF net subscriptions for multiple consecutive days =
   incremental capital; sustained premium >= 1% = retail rushing in (top signal).

### Dimension B: Price action

1. **Sector index breakout**: sector index (Shenwan tier 1/2, THS concept, EastMoney industry) breaks
   above the prior 6-month high + weekly green candle + volume = launch signal.
2. **Limit-up clustering**: same sector with >= 5 limit-ups in a single day = sector anomaly; >= 10 =
   mainstream direction. Must hold for 2-3 consecutive days to count as a real launch.
3. **Consecutive-board height**: leader on >= 3 boards = fermentation expected; leader breaks the board
   = ebb precursor.
4. **Sector RS (relative strength)**: sector index 30-day return ranks within Shenwan tier-1 top 5 =
   capital is focused here.
5. **Volume share**: sector's daily turnover >= 10% of total market turnover for 3 consecutive sessions
   = main-line confirmed.
6. **Second/third-tier response**: after the leader limit-ups, second-tier names in the sector follow
   the next day with >= 5% gains = sector effect holds. If only 1-2 names move = single-stock action,
   not sector action.

### Dimension C: Theme catalyst (narrative)

A true hot sector must have a clear "why now" narrative. No narrative ≈ pure speculation ≈ one-day wonder.

Catalyst types:

1. **Policy catalyst**: State Council / NDRC / MIIT / MoF heavyweight document, industry plan, subsidy
   program. Policy calendar: March two sessions, July Politburo meeting, October fourth plenum,
   December Central Economic Work Conference (CEWC).
2. **Industry event**: leader's technology breakthrough, mass-production milestone, large overseas
   order, patent announcement.
3. **Overseas mapping**:
   - NVIDIA / OpenAI major release -> domestic compute, AI applications
   - Tesla Optimus progress -> A-share humanoid robotics
   - SpaceX launch -> commercial spaceflight
   - Micron / Samsung price hikes -> domestic memory
   - Escalation of Western sanctions -> indigenous-control (semis, OS)
4. **Earnings-driven**: leader earnings preannouncement beats -> sector follows (especially supply-chain
   up/downstream).
5. **Cycle-driven**: commodity breakout (copper, aluminum, rare earth, oil & gas) -> upstream resource
   stocks.
6. **Major meeting expectation**: pre-meeting front-running (trading expectations), post-meeting
   realization or invalidation (trading delivery / killing expectations).

### Dimension D: Sentiment & buzz

Mainly used to judge "position", not "direction".

Positive (launch) signals:
- Xueqiu / EastMoney forum discussion rising but not maxed out
- WeChat index / Baidu index trending up moderately
- Sell-side starts releasing deep reports on the sector densely

Reverse (top) signals — collect them all and you reduce:
- Front-page financial media (Cailianshe, Xinhua Finance, Securities Times) bullish for consecutive days
- Forum heat list has >= 5 names from this sector
- Cab drivers / coworkers / friends spontaneously talk about it
- WeChat index spikes >= 5x
- Mutual funds launch themed funds in cluster (classic top signal)

### Dimension E: Supply-chain verification (slowest but most concrete)

Capital can lie, prices can lie — supply-chain data cannot (and is hardest to fake).

- **Upstream price & volume**: raw materials, key components rising or in shortage (e.g. HBM, optical
  module chips, rare earth).
- **Mid-stream orders**: industry association monthly data, customs import/export, listed companies'
  monthly operating reports, tender announcements.
- **Downstream verification**: end-market sales data (NEV, AI server shipments), overseas vendor guidance.
- **Fundamental inflection**: industry-wide ROE / gross margin turning up.

**Supply-chain verification lags capital flow, but once confirmed = main-up wave can sustain 6-18
months.** A pure capital-driven rally without supply-chain confirmation lasts at most 1-3 months.

## 4. Scanning SOP (fixed daily / weekly routine)

### 4.1 Pre-market scan (daily 8:30-9:15, 15 min)

1. **Overnight overseas**: Nasdaq, NVIDIA, Tesla, Micron + commodities (copper, oil, rare earth). Map
   potentially-affected A-share sectors.
2. **Overnight policy / major events**: Cailianshe wires, Xinhua Finance headlines. Identify any black
   swans or new main-line catalysts.
3. **Morning sell-side**: quickly skim morning reports (don't rely; just check for any consensus direction).
4. **Personal watchlist alerts**: check pre-market filings for watchlist names (earnings preannouncements,
   insider selling, regulatory action).

### 4.2 Intraday scan (10:00, 11:00, 14:00, 14:50 — 5 min each)

Don't watch the live tape. Glance only at these four checkpoints:

1. **10:00**: 30 min after open, is the top-5 sector ranking stable (avoid being fooled by the opening pulse)?
2. **11:00**: before end of morning session — limit-up count, top-board height, breadth of sector rally.
3. **14:00**: after afternoon open, any "afternoon-fermentation" new sector (common A-share late-session anomaly)?
4. **14:50**: closing 10 min — board-sealing status, did the leader break its board, are institutional seats grabbing?

### 4.3 Post-close review (daily 16:00-17:00, 30-60 min)

Fixed template (in order):

1. **Three indices**: CSI300, ChiNext, SSE Composite close -> update framed.md §1 index & sentiment gate.
2. **Sector return ranking** (THS concept + Shenwan tier-1): record top-5 gainers + limit-up count per sector.
3. **Limit-up board analysis**: aggregate by sector — identify "mainstream direction", "catch-up direction",
   "divergent direction".
4. **Capital flow**:
   - Northbound daily net inflow + top-5 industries + top-10 names by net buy
   - Main-force capital top-5 sector net inflows
   - LHB institutional seat scan (focus: consecutive-board leaders, breakout names on volume)
5. **Tracking sheet update**: add today's row to the sector tracking sheet; drop ebbed sectors (see §6 template).
6. **Tomorrow's plan**: identify 1-2 directions likely to ferment further; list their leaders with entry /
   stop prices.

### 4.4 Weekend deep review (Saturday, 2-3 hours)

1. **Weekly sector RS ranking**: Shenwan tier-1 sector 13-week / 26-week return ranking changes.
2. **Policy calendar — next week**: upcoming important meetings / document expectations.
3. **Earnings window check**: which key companies report next week.
4. **Overseas mapping recap**: this week's key NVIDIA / Tesla / OpenAI moments.
5. **Portfolio review**: check each holding against framed.md §4 exit rules; structure still intact (above the
   5-day MA, no time-stop / price-stop hit)? trim needed?
6. **Watchlist update**: update the watchlistd.md pool (max 6 names).

## 5. Real vs fake hot sectors: 4 corroborations

Satisfy >= 3 = real main line; <= 2 = fake heat / theme play, don't participate.

| Dimension | Real hot | Fake hot |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Persistence | Sustained capital inflow >= 5 trading days | 1-2 day pulse, breaks down day 3 |
| Breadth | >= 10 stocks in sector responding (>= 5% gains) | Only leader 1-2 names move |
| Capital nature | At least 2 of {northbound, mutual fund, institutional LHB seats} confirm | Pure hot-money seats, no northbound, no institutions |
| Narrative quality | Clear policy / industry / overseas catalyst chain | "Theme play", "ride the heat", no substance |

**Two extra exclusion signals** (any one -> abandon):

- Sector leader prints a "ceiling-to-floor" (open at +10% limit, close at -10% limit) or "spike-and-crash"
  (open gap +10%, close -10%).
- Front-page media coverage for 3 consecutive days + mutual fund launches new themed fund = already in
  climax stage; **do NOT enter**.

## 7. Leader identification: from sector to stock

After confirming a sector as a real hot, lock the leader in this order:

### 7.1 Leader ranking priority

1. **First to limit-up / first to break prior high** (sentiment leader)
2. **Pure on both earnings AND theme** (fundamental leader): earnings beat + 100% of main business
   maps to this theme
3. **Free-float market cap in 5B-50B CNY band** (capital-friendly): too small = manipulable, too large
   = won't move
4. **LHB institutional + northbound show up together** (capital alignment)
5. **Highest consecutive-board count + earliest board-seal + moderate turnover (3-15%)**

### 7.2 Quick "leader vs follower" filter

| Feature | Leader | Follower |
|---------|--------|----------|
| Time to limit-up | Pre-10:00 morning session | Afternoon, even late close |
| Sealing depth | Thick (seal-order / float >= 1%) | Thin, repeatedly opens |
| Earnings | Verified / strong expectation | Riding the concept, main business unrelated |
| Historical position | Already a sub-sector leader before | A name that "appeared from nowhere" |

**Don't buy followers**: their board-break rate is materially higher than leaders, and even when the
sector continues, followers' gains lag the leader meaningfully.

### 7.3 Run candidates through framed.md

After locking 1-2 candidate leaders, run each through framed.md:

- §1 gate -> index & sentiment regime must be green (not ebb / freeze) before any entry
- §2 hard filters -> free-float cap 5B-50B, volume present, theme leader / solid #2; exclude ST, 5+ consecutive
  boards, pre-earnings names
- §3 entry trigger -> a defined trigger must exist: pullback to 5/10-day MA holding on volume, OR platform
  breakout on >= 1.5x average volume
- §5 sizing -> 2-slot concentrated book: ~25,000 RMB per slot (~50% of account each),
  full 50K deployable. Risk on each slot is governed by the ATR-based stop width
  (framed.md §4A), not a 1% cap. Also check liquidity: position ≤ 1% of 10-day
  average turnover.

Pass -> add to watchlistd.md; enter on the §3 trigger, not before.

## 8. 6 common A-share hot-sector driver templates

Each template has a distinct entry cadence and duration; identifying the template helps you set a
reasonable take-profit / stop-loss horizon.

### Template 1: Policy catalyst

- Trigger: State Council / ministry releases a document, plan, subsidy program
- Cadence: sector erupts on document-release day, main-up over 1-2 weeks, differentiation within 1-3 months
- Examples: low-altitude economy, new-quality productive forces, humanoid robotics support policy
- Duration: 1-6 months (depends on policy strength + supply-chain delivery)
- Entry: 1-3 trading days after the document, when the leader prints a §3B platform breakout on volume
- Exit: policy expectation realized + media consensus bullish + mutual funds launch themed funds

### Template 2: Earnings-driven

- Trigger: leader's earnings preannouncement / report substantially beats
- Cadence: gap up on announcement day, 3-5 days confirm main-up, supply chain follows within 1 month
- Examples: historical cases — CATL, Luxshare, NAURA
- Duration: 3-12 months (fundamentals-driven; longest)
- Entry: post-announcement pullback holds + weekly breakout on volume
- Exit: earnings growth inflects (growth rate declines 2 consecutive quarters)

### Template 3: Overseas mapping

- Trigger: overseas leader (NVIDIA / Tesla / OpenAI / SpaceX) major announcement
- Cadence: overseas releases at night; A-share gaps up next morning; ferments 2-5 days; pulls back in 1-2 weeks
- Examples: NVIDIA GTC -> compute sector; Tesla AI Day -> robotics; SpaceX launch -> commercial space
- Duration: 1-3 months (sentiment-driven)
- Entry: don't chase the gap day; wait 3-5 days for the leader to relaunch on a low-volume pullback + volume expansion
- Exit: overseas name pulls back + A-share leader breaks board

### Template 4: Cycle-driven

- Trigger: commodity breakout (copper, aluminum, rare earth, oil & gas) or upstream material price hikes
- Cadence: commodity leads by 1-2 weeks; equities follow with delay then accelerate
- Examples: historical moves — Zijin Mining, China Northern Rare Earth, CMOC
- Duration: 3-12 months (depends on commodity cycle length)
- Entry: commodity breaks 60-week MA + leader's weekly chart prints volume
- Exit: commodity tops out + sector shows distribution characteristics

### Template 5: Indigenous-control / sanctions-driven

- Trigger: sanctions escalation, tech export controls tightened, diplomatic friction
- Cadence: gap up on news day; ferments 2-3 days; differentiates over 1-2 weeks
- Examples: Huawei supply chain, semiconductor equipment, OS, IT-innovation
- Duration: 3-12 months (structural theme; reactivates repeatedly)
- Entry: post-news pullback holds + leader makes new high
- Exit: news softens + theme cools

### Template 6: Major-meeting expectation

- Trigger: two sessions, CEWC, Politburo meeting, third plenum
- Cadence: pre-meeting 2-4 weeks trade expectations; during meeting realize or invalidate
- Examples: pre-CEWC "stable growth"; pre-two-sessions "new infrastructure"
- Duration: 1-2 months (event-driven; shortest)
- Entry: position pre-meeting (high risk), OR post-meeting pick the "most clearly benefiting" direction
- Exit: 1-2 weeks after the meeting ends — expectations realized, exit

## 9. Entry timing: sector stage x framed.md §3 trigger matrix

|                                               | Incubation  | Launch          | Main-up          | Climax      | Ebb         |
|-----------------------------------------------|-------------|-----------------|------------------|-------------|-------------|
| **§3B platform / prior-high breakout on vol** | No          | **Best entry**  | OK, tighter stop | No          | No          |
| **§3A pullback to 5/10-day MA holds on vol**  | No          | **Second best** | OK               | No          | No          |
| **§3C strong-leader relay (half size)**       | No          | Warm-up only    | Warm-up, riskier | No          | No          |
| **Consecutive board (5+)**                    | Don't chase | Don't chase     | Don't chase      | Don't chase | Don't chase |

**Core principle**: enter only when the sector is in "Launch" (or early "Main-up") AND the stock fires a
framed.md §3 trigger — §3B platform / prior-high breakout on >= 1.5x volume is the cleanest, §3A pullback to
the 5/10-day MA holding on renewed volume is second. §3C relay is half-size, warm-up only. Never chase 5+
consecutive boards (framed.md §2C auto-exclusion); "Climax / Ebb" = no new entries.

## 10. Early identification of sector ebb (leading signals)

Sector ebb typically leads the sector-index breakdown by 1-2 weeks; spotting it early avoids getting
trapped in the late stage of main-up:

1. **Leader breaks the board**: sector leader drops from limit-up to -5% or worse = end of main-up.
2. **Consecutive-board height collapses**: max board count drops from 5-7 to 2-3, and new consecutive-board
   names show distorted high turnover.
3. **Second-tier follow-through quality drops**: previously "leader limit-up -> second tier +7%",
   becomes "leader limit-up -> second tier +2%".
4. **Limit-down / failed-board count rises**: sector has >= 3 failed boards in a day = capital disagreement
   is clear.
5. **Northbound starts outflow**: northbound flips from net buy to net sell >= 300M CNY for 3 consecutive days.
6. **Media euphoria**: front-page Cailianshe / CCTV Finance / mutual funds launching themed fund — all
   cluster on this theme.
7. **Retail searches spike**: Baidu index / WeChat index doubles within 5 days.
8. **Theme extends to "extreme concept-riding"**: even names whose main business is totally unrelated
   limit up = last party before capital exhaustion.

**When 6 / 7 / 8 appear together, it's almost 100% a top.**

## 11. Risk red lines (sectors / setups not to participate in)

1. **"Ceiling-to-floor" leader**: opens at limit-up then closes at limit-down -> sector sentiment reversal;
   no entries for 3-5 days.
2. **Open after consecutive high one-line limit-ups (>= 5 boards)**: hot-money game; retail relay = catching
   knives; absolute no-touch.
3. **Sector index already up >= 50% and >= 3 months since launch**: main-up is past halfway; reward too thin.
4. **Front-page media + mutual fund launches themed fund + retail mania — all three present** = top; trim
   only, no new entries.
5. **Pure speculation on unrealized policy expectation**: no new entries the last week before the meeting
   (realization risk high).
6. **Stock-level**: ST / *ST / under investigation / goodwill impairment warning / major shareholder selling
   -> no entry even if sector is hot.
7. **Index regime tier 3** (distribution days >= 6, iVX > 32) -> no participation in any hot theme; cash is king.

## 14. Personal review & iteration

In the last week of each month, do a "sector-identification ability" self-check:

1. **Hit rate**: of the N hot sectors you identified this month, how many actually entered main-up?
   Target >= 60%.
2. **Miss rate**: of the real main-up sectors this month, how many did you miss? Target <= 20%.
3. **False-positive rate**: how many times did you enter and find it was fake heat? Target <= 1/month.
4. **Exit timing**: how many days did you delay exiting after the climax / ebb signal appeared?
   Target <= 3 days.

For any dimension below target, strengthen specifically next month (e.g. miss rate high -> intensify
pre-market overseas scan; false-positive rate high -> tighten §5 corroboration standards).

## Appendix A: Common pitfalls

1. **"The sector is going up, I'll chase"**: ignores stage; chasing into climax / ebb guarantees a bag.
2. **"Leader has run too much; second tier offers value"**: A-share second tier elasticity is far below
   the leader, and more prone to board-break.
3. **"The sector logic is solid; I'll add on dips"**: sector logic unchanged ≠ trend unchanged; once trend
   breaks, even the strongest logic yields.
4. **"This sector erupted last time; let's go again"**: a sector's second launch is much less likely than
   a new sector's first, and demands stricter corroboration.
5. **"Policy is out — I'll rush in"**: policy-release day is often the expectations peak; wait for the
   pullback to verify.
6. **"Mutual funds are adding; I'll follow"**: mutual fund disclosure lags 1-3 months; following them
   often means buying the climax.
