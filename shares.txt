Examples on the standard account:
* USD $4,166 trade -> minimum kicks in -> USD $5.00
* USD $10,000 trade -> 0.12% -> USD $12.00
* USD $50,000 trade -> 0.12% -> USD $60.00

Foreign Exchange (FX) fee — the big one:
* 0.55% per currency conversion (AUD <-> USD).
* Charged on the way in when funding the trade in USD, and on the way out when selling and converting back to AUD.
* A round-trip therefore costs ~1.10% in FX margin, on top of brokerage.
* The Plus tier lets you hold USD locally and skip FX on subsequent trades — only worth it for frequent traders.

Other CommSec-side costs:
* Custody fee: AUD $0 (was previously charged, now waived).
* Inactivity fee: AUD $0.
* Real-time market data: AUD $0.
* US regulatory pass-throughs (SEC fee on sells, FINRA TAF) — small, "at cost", typically a few cents to a few dollars.

Practical takeaway:
* Single buy from AUD on standard account: brokerage ($5 min or 0.12%) + 0.55% FX one way.
* Round-trip (buy + sell + convert back to AUD): ~$10 brokerage + ~1.10% FX.
* FX margin dominates total cost on small-to-mid trades. IBKR is ~0.002% FX vs CommSec 0.55%.
